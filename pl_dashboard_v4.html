HOW THIS MODEL WORKS

Most football prediction models make a fundamental error: they treat historical form as equally reliable whether it's from last week or three seasons ago. This model uses a progressive blending system — at the start of the season, predictions lean heavily on multi-year ELO ratings because you have no current data. By GW31, 82% of the form signal comes from this season alone. The model adapts its own confidence as evidence accumulates.

The 5,000 Monte Carlo simulations are what separate a "who will win" prediction from a probabilistic forecast. Each simulation independently draws match outcomes from the model's probability distributions and tallies a final table. Arsenal winning 99.3% of simulations isn't a statement of certainty — it's a statement that in virtually every plausible path through the remaining 7 games, Arsenal finish top, given their 9-point lead.

Walk-forward validation is the only honest performance measure for a time-series model. Training on 2021-25 and testing on 2025-26 would be data leakage. Instead, each validation fold trains only on the past and predicts the future — the same constraint the model faces in production.

ELO + decay

Standard Elo with 10% seasonal regression. K=20, home advantage=100. Decay prevents a team's 2022 peak inflating their 2026 rating.

Progressive form blend

Weight = SeasonProgress × current-season EWMA + (1-SP) × long-run EWMA. Shifts from historical prior to live evidence as the season unfolds.

Market signal

Bookmaker implied probabilities (overround removed) are the single most informative feature. Markets encode injury news, team selection, and analyst consensus that public stats don't capture.

Monte Carlo simulation

5,000 independent season completions. Each remaining match drawn from predicted H/D/A probabilities. Title probability = fraction of simulations won.
